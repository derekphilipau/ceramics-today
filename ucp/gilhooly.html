Up Close and Personal mini interview

David Gilhooly

Up Close and Personal - an opportunity to get to know ceramists and studio potters better. Our current interviewee is California-based ceramist David Gilhooly.

Renown ceramist David Gilhooly was at the forefront of the development of San Francisco Bay Area Funk in the mid 1960s, along with Robert Arneson, Chris Untersteher, Margaret Dodd and Peter Vandenberge. He became well known for his ‘ Frog Pots’ and sculptures, all dealing with an imaginary ‘ Frog World’ , which, as he says, oddly paralleled our own. In the early 1980s he became obsessed with food, dealing in his sculpture with our excesses and fascinations with eatables. Gilhooly finally gave up clay in 1996 to work on what he calls his 'shadow boxes'.

 

Q. What is your favorite novel?
A. Snow Crash by Neal Stephenson though this changes all the time. I also reread all of Eiji Yoshikawa's novels from time to time especially Musashi and Rudy Rucker's novels especially those in the " Ware" series and reread all the William Gibson novels in order Neuromancer and on. I also have an especial fondness for the novels of Gabriel Garcia Marquez.

Q. What is your favorite non-fiction book?
A. M.F.K. Fisher's The Art of Eating (a compilation of five of her books) or any of the autobiographical books by Emily Carr. Rats, Lice and History is also fun.

Q. What are you reading at the moment?
A. Neal Stephenson's Quicksilver

Q. What is your favorite movie?
A. Another tough to answer question which deserves multiple answers. Takeshi Kitano's Sonatine; Amelie; Mike's Dead or Alive #1; Killer Klowns from Outer Space and Armageddon. Right now I have been watching repeatedly Hero with Jet Li.

Q. Your most favorite CD or piece of music?
A. Insane Clown Posse's Hell's Pit; Laibach's Jesus Christ Superstars; Miles Davis' On the Corner; Shostakovich's 7th Symphony (especially reccomended when throwing); The Congos' Heart of the Congos; and for a single song Klaus Nomui singing 'Lightening Strikes'.

Q. Your favorite food?
A. Northern Chinese Cooking

Q. What do you like to drink?
A. Chardonnay

Q. If you could own any piece of ceramic, what would it be?
A. A Tang Dynasty Hilltop jar

Q. Your favorite ceramic artist or potter?
A. Chris Unterseher

Q. To you, what is the meaning or function of art?
A. It is the highest quality communication there is and pulls a culture along with it into the future. Without art there is no future for a society.

Q. What have been the most influential events in your life?
A. Going to the University of California at Davis as an anthropology student and meeting a cute art major and taking a Wayne Thiebaud class and Arneson's first class ever offered to impress her. But during the summer before the classes started she left school, got married and I never saw her again.

Q. What do you love?
A. Working

Q. What inspires you the most?
A. Other people's work especially William Wiley and Peter Saul

Q. What is your greatest regret?
A. I have none

Q. Your greatest achievement?
A. Doing what I want to do rather than what people would like me to do and
still surviving just fine.

 

Many thanks to David Gilhooly for the above mini interview. Please visit David's website to see his latest work!
