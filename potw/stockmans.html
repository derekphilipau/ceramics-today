Featured Artist  

Piet Stockmans
Belgian ceramic designer

"Creation is the result of activity and not of thinking. It is activity that generates ideas which, themselves, give rise to other ones. It is a process in the course of which decisive choices are made in a mysterious way. It is the automatism with which the farmer ploughs the fields, a phenomenon that can be compared to the way prayers are used, mantras recited or everyday gestures repeated. It is a quest for simplicity, peace, physical well-being."

Piet Stockmans is a Belgian ceramist and designer. From 1966-89 Stockmans worked with the Dutch company Royal Mosa, where he designed the world’s most ubiquitous coffee cup, ‘Sonja’. From 1969-98 he taught industrial design at the Institute of Higher Education in Limburg and from 1983-85 he was Professor of Ceramic Design at the Academy of Industrial Design in Eindhoven. Since 1989 he has worked as a freelance designer. In 1998 he was the recipient of the prestigious Henri van der Velde prize for outstanding career achievement. Throughout his design career, Stockmans has also successfully exhibited one-of-a-kind ceramics and ceramic installations using thousands of multiples. He has limited his palette to a white porcelain used in combination with a blue slip, however this has not limited his prolific output nor his creativity.
