Featured Artist  

Kato Takuro
Japanese studio potter

Japanese studio potter Kato Takuro (1898-1985) took over the family kiln in 1914 at the age of 16. He made vessels in several traditional Japanese styles, including those of Mino, Oribe and Shigaraki.

In 1933 he published a book on old Seto ware (Ki-Seto) in which he questioned the traditional belief that Ki-Seto was actually fired in Seto. This led to the infamous Ki-Seto book burning incident of the same year. His thesis has since been proven to be correct.

From 1934 K. published in several volumes Genshoku Toki Dai Jiten, Japan's only comprehensive dictionary of oriental ceramics.

In 1937 he was involved in another incident, the ‘Einin no Tsubo Scandal’, in which he duped the well known pottery scholar Fujio Koyama (1900-1975) into believing some copies he had made of Einin period (1293-1299) sake bottles were actually ancient wares.

The Ishikawa Prefectural Museum in Kanazawa celebrated the centennial of Kato’s birth with an exhibition of 160 of his masterpieces. His work is on permanent display at the Suishoen Togei Memorial Museum in Nagoya.
