Featured Artist  

John Dermer
Australian studio potter.

Australian studio potter John Dermer studied Art and Design at the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology (RMIT) University, then traveled to England to work with various potters, including two influential months with Michael Leach. There, he was also artist-in-residence at the famous Wedgwood works. He returned to Australia, establishing a studio in 1973.

Dermer makes wheel-thrown and salt-glazed porcelain and stoneware vessels, some with terra sigillata and others with oriental style glazes such as copper-reds and celadons. Many of his vessels display a characteristic fluting.

His work is represented in Australia's Parliament House, the Federal High Court Building, National State and Regional Galleries, as well as numerous private collections. His studio 'Kirby's Flat Pottery' is located near Australia's historic town of Yackandandah.
