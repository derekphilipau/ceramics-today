Terracotta vessel (Shango odo), detail, No. 66.1.14, 15 3/4" x 13", Ita Yemoo Museum of Yoruba Pottery, Ile Ife, Nigeria. The potter (always a woman) applied moist plastic clay to create images of lighting, earthworms, turtles, and other messengers of ashe (vital force). The potter also modeled chiefly scepters - an ideoplastic allusion to the inherent nobility of those who are receptacles of divine power (ashe). Such vessels were commissioned and paid for by devotees of Shango. Photo by Ron du Bois, 1988.
