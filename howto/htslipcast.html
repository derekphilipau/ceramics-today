How to Slipcast Ceramics

Slipcasting is a widespread ceramic technique, suitable to the manufacturing of multiples. A plaster mold is required.

Difficulty Level: average      Time Required: 60 min

Here's How:
  1. If your mold consists of several pieces, tie them together with thick rubber bands or something suitable to the molds size.
  2. Stir your slip thoroughly with a clean stick, but without introducing air bubbles into the liquid.
  3. If you have a very large bucket of slip, pour an amount into a smaller container, which will hold enough slip to fill your mold.
  4. Pour the slip through a sieve into your dry mold. (Straining the slip eliminates any lumps from getting through, which would otherwise stick to the wall of your pot.)
  5. You will be able to observe the slip adhering to the mold and thickening. At this stage it is possible to gently knock the sides of the mold (let's say with the ball of your hand or with a rubber mallet) to free any air bubbles which may be trapped in the clay. These will rise to the surface.
  6. As the clay wall thickens, water is absorbed by the plaster and the level of the slip will drop. Continue pouring small amounts of slip into the mold to top up the level to the top edge.
  7. When you think that the correct wall thickness is reached, pour all the slip out of your mold back into your bucket. It is possible to time the period the slip stays in the mold, but this will vary in subsequent casts, depending on the moisture content of the mold.
  8. Depending on the thickness of your clay walls and the moisture content of the mold, the ceramic object will be dry enough to remove after several hours.
  9. As the clay will shrink, whereas the plaster will not, the ceramic form will shrink from the mold and 'pop out', if the form allows it to.
Tips:
  1. Make sure you have enough slip to fill your mold.
  2. Have an extra empty bucket handy.
  3. Clay and plaster do not mix -- make sure not to get plaster into your slip!
