How to make the Best Ever Pizza Dough

Pizza dough? But, this is the ceramics site! But many of us are aware of the similarities between ceramics and cooking, e.g. kneading clay & dough; rolling clay & dough; baking & firing, etc. Well, here is my very own secret recipe for a no-fail, great pizza dough!

Difficulty Level: easy      Time Required: 90 min.

Here's How:
  1. Preheat your oven
  2. Heat a cup of water to body temperature.
  3. Put a pound (half a kilo) of flour (white or white/wholemeal mix) in a ceramic or glass bowl.
  4. Add a teaspoon of salt and two tablespoons of sugar to the dough. Mix thoroughly (dry).
  5. Make a depression in the dough and add about a tablespoon of dried yeast. Fresh yeast can also be used (use more).
  6. Slowly add the warm (but not hot) water while stirring with a fork or wooden spoon.
  7. Keep turning over the dough until most of the dough is mixed.
  8. Put some flour on your hands and knead the dough. Add more flour to the dough in small increments if it sticks to your fingers.
  9. When the dough is sufficiently kneaded, cover the bowl with a clean tea towel and place in the middle of the preheated oven.
  10. Turn off the oven and leave the dough to rise for about one hour.
  11. When the dough has risen, remove it from the oven and re-knead with a bit more flour (if still sticky). It's ready to roll!
Tips:
  1. Before putting the dough in the oven. Repeatedly stick a fork in both sides. This introduces air into the dough, for the yeast to convert the sugar.
  2. Make sure you put in the sugar. This will not make the dough sweet -- the yeast feeds on it, creating carbondioxide and making the dough rise!
  3. Make sure neither your water, nor the oven is too hot, otherwise the yeast will be killed off.
  4. If you are using a moist topping, like freshly cut tomatos, brush the rolled out dough with olive oil. This will stop it from going soggy.
