akène
A international internet portal for the visual arts, it was created to awaken enthusiasm for the applied arts and to present outstanding works to an international public. A jury of recognised experts from museums, galleries and art schools as well as artists working in the field selects the applicants. Akène presents not only Classics of applied art, but also the Avant-Garde and particularly Fine Art, including ceramics. German/English

Autio, Rudi
Rudy Autio is one of the most masterful and influential artists working with clay in the United States today. He headed the ceramics area at the University of Montana for twenty-eight years and is now retired as Professor Emeritus of the School of Fine Arts.

Barash, Shulamit ('Shula')
Ceramic sculptor Shulamit Barash was born in Ramat-Hasharon in 1943. She has lived in Ashdod since 1963. She is a member of the Israel Painters and Sculptors Association, as well as that of Ashdod.

Beard, Peter
Strong simple shapes are decorated with complex glaze surfaces to complement and excite the eye. Landscape in all forms is my main source of inspiration, along with the art of ancient Egypt. Combinations of shiny, matt and semi-matt glazes are built up in layers to create the textural surfaces during firing.

Behan, Jim
Jim Behan's one-person studio is in Carlow, Ireland.

Bellero, Lauren
For me, there is immense pleasure, excitement and serenity in creating with clay. My hope is to pass on a simple pleasure to those who also feel drawn to this ancient and inviting connection to the earth. Using a variety of decorating and firing techniques, I create hand-built and wheel-thrown stoneware pottery with reckless abandon! The result is one-of-a-kind decorative and functional pieces. Located in Red Bank, NJ, USA.

Beresford, Francesz
Frances works in North Lincolnshire. Inspired by animals and with a fantasy theme she works in porcelain, smoke-fired china and earthenware. She paints in oils and produces murals on tiles.
