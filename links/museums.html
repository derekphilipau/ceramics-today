Arizona State University Art Museum
Established in 1950, ASU Art Museum is recognized as one of the Southwest’s most significant contemporary art institutions. In addition to serving students and scholars, ASU Art Museum is a cultural hub for the Phoenix Metropolitan area.

Art Institute of Chicago
A large collection of Tribal African to Modern Art, including many ceramic works.

Canadian Museum of Civilization
The earliest ceramics in Canada were made in the northern Yukon more than 3,500 years ago, within sight of the Arctic Ocean. Find out more about the history of Native ceramic making traditions in Canada.

Ceramics Research Center at the ASU Art Museum

Museo Richard-Ginori della Manifattura di Doccia
The most important porcelain factory in Italy, established in 1737 and still active to the present day. The Museum holds a remarkable collection (more than 8.000 pieces) of Ginori and Richard-Ginori porcelain and tin-glazed earthenware, dating form 1737 to 1990. Among the highlights of the collection are impressive life-size sculptures of the early period, like the Medici Venus or Love and Psyche ( about 1750) and the largest existing collection of Art Deco Ceramics by the world famous designer Gio Ponti. The Museum is just six miles outside of Florence, in a little town called Sesto Fiorentino.

Museu del Cantir d'Argentona
The Argentona Pottery Water Vessels Museum, located in Argentona, near Barcelona (Spain) is the first and foremost in its specialty, has as its primary objective the collection, conservation, study and propagation of all vessels related to the use of water, irrelevant of their geographic origin, period of production, material or typology.

Racine Art Museum
The Racine Art Museum (RAM) will soon be the home of one of the most significant collections of contemporary craft in North America. Focusing on original expressions in ceramics, fibers, glass, metals and wood from internationally recognized artists, the new museum will present major exhibitions along with the permanent collection of the Charles A. A teaching and research facility, one educational component of the New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University.
