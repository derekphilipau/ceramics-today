Previous (A-K)

La Meridiana
A centre for seminars and workshops on specific ceramic topics. Courses are held by ceramic artists of international recognition coming from various countries.

Lakeside Pottery
Lakeside Pottery is a ceramic school, studio and gallery in Stamford, Connecticut, run by Morty Bachar. Classes for adults and children, workshops, open studio membership and ceramic / clay team building workshops are available.

Missouri University Clay Klub
Upcoming workshops, virtual gallery, salt kiln page, Woodfiring workshop.

Montana State University Ceramics
In addition to a 3,400 square foot open studio, the ceramics area includes graduate studio space, kiln room, damp room, clay and glazing room, and a large work yard with wood, salt, and raku kilns. The equipment in the main studio consists of fifteen wheels, wedging tables, a Brent slab roller, extruder, and two slip machines.

Mother Earth Clay Art Center
A modern, 10,000 square-foot facility in Sunnyvale, California offers memberships, classes, workshops, retail sales of tools and materials, and a gallery of members' work.

New York State College of Ceramics
With the Scholes Library of Ceramics, International Museum of Ceramic Art, Art & Design pages, Engineering & Materials pages.

NY/NJ Academy of Ceramic Arts
Ceramic artist Frank Bosco and his wife, Ann, founded the Academy in the spring of 1997. The school is dedicated to the education and creative growth of individuals who enjoy working with clay.

Northern Virginia Community College
CVarious courses in Alexandria, Virginia.

Ohio University School of Art
Ceramics department run by Brad Schweiger. The Ceramics program emphasizes the realization of individual goals. We encourage the exploration of ideas and materials to foster an atmosphere of diversity.

Oregon College of Arts and Crafts
Embodying both a history as a traditional crafts school and the drive toward innovation, OCAC ceramics grounds students in basic building, finishing and firing techniques. Concurrently, students work with clay as both a sculptural and functional medium.

Otago Polytechnic School of Art
Probably the best ceramics department in New Zealand and the only NZ school offerring full time ceramics to BFA and MFA levels.

Richmond Art Center
offers classes and workshops in ceramics, among other media. In addition there are many children's classes to choose from. Richmond Art Center exhibits contemporary emerging artists in its galleries. (Northern Ca.)

San José State University
with Stan Welsh, Professor and Monica Van Den Dool, Lecturer. Offers students at all levels an opportunity to explore the potential for clay as a medium for developing individual direction. Instructors teach a wide range of technical skills and building techniques in combination with conceptual development.

Southern Cross University
The Ceramics studio offers learning at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. International applications are encouraged. Specialisations are broad, but include woodfiring. Full time staff - Tony Nankervis & Patsy Hely.

Sturt Craft Center
Sturt is a multi disciplinary crafts education center in Mittagong, New South Wales, Australia.

Sydney College of the Arts
Offers a whole range of courses from the undergraduate BFA with honors year to postgraduate coursework and research degrees, all with ceramics concentrations. Included is a Master of studio Arts, a Master of contemporary Art for Educators, a Master of visual Arts and a PhD.

Universities & College Degree Shows in Britain
This page is dedicated to College and Universities degree Shows with Ceramics in 2001.

University of Art & Design Helsinki, Ceramic & Glass Design Department
an appealing web design, and interesting and beautifully photographed ceramics and glass works.

University of Dundee
Ceramics by students at the University of Dundee (degree show).

University of Wales, Aberystwyth
The Ceramic Collection and Archive at the University of Wales, Aberystwyth, with the Ceramic Archive, Research info and more.

University of Southern California
Introductory and advanced course in ceramics, under Professor and Department Chair Ken Price.

Watershed: Center for Ceramic Art
Watershed is a residency/retreat which provides artists with time and space to create in clay. Located in Newcastle/Edgecomb, Maine, USA.

William Schran
Bill Schran describes the ceramics courses offered at the Alexandria Campus of Northern Virginia Community College, provides links to other ceramics web sites and lists glaze formulas used in the studio.
